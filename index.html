How Does It Work?

Aerozen, a cutting-edge air purification system, is poised to transform the landscape of air quality in Bangladesh. Designed to combat the pervasive issue of air pollution, Aerozen operates by drawing in polluted air, subjecting it to advanced filtration within a protective glass layer, and then infusing it with fresh oxygen generated by trees. This innovative process yields a substantial increase in oxygen levels, resulting in the production of high-quality, oxygenated air. With Aerozen, not only is the air purified, but it is also enriched with vital oxygen, promoting healthier living environments and addressing the pressing need for cleaner air in Bangladesh.

Explanation of The Process:

1. Polluted Air Intake: The air purifier starts by drawing in the polluted air from the surroundings. This air often contains a mixture of harmful particles and pollutants, contributing to poor air quality.

2. Filtration and Storage: Once the polluted air is captured, it undergoes thorough filtration to remove contaminants such as dust, smoke, and other airborne particles. The purified air is then stored within a protective layer made of glass. This glass-based storage ensures that the purified air remains free from recontamination until it is released.

3. Oxygenation Process: In addition to purifying the air, your air purifier employs an innovative process to enhance its oxygen content. Fresh air generated by trees, which naturally produce oxygen through photosynthesis, is introduced into the system. This fresh oxygen is then mixed with the purified air stored in the glass layer.

4. Generation of Oxygenated Air: Through this unique blending process, the air purifier produces a significant quantity of oxygenated air. By combining the benefits of air purification with the natural oxygenation provided by trees, the device generates a supply of high-quality, breathable air that is rich in oxygen.
